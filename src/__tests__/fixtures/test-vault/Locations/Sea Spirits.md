# Sea Spirits

Ancient lore holds that the souls of drowned sailors linger near the wharves
after dark, drawn to the warmth of lanterns and the smell of tar. Fishermen
call them the Watchers — not dangerous, but unsettling. A candle left in a
window is considered both an invitation and an appeasement.

## Signs of Their Presence

Cold air that moves against the wind. Ropes that untie themselves overnight.
The sound of oars when no boat is visible. Experienced dockhands consider
these ordinary and pay them no mind; newcomers rarely sleep soundly in
their first week near the water.

## Offerings and Appeasement

A small clay figurine cast into the water at the autumn equinox keeps the
Watchers docile through winter. The tradition predates the city's founding;
nobody knows who started it.
